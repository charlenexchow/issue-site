What is food waste?
Food waste is the loss of food at any point along the food supply chain, from production to processing to retail to consumption. Waste can occur for various reasons such as overproduction, spoilage, or consumer behaviors. This phenomena is a global issue with detrimental social, economical, and environmental impacts. There are many possible efforts to reduce food waste from an individual consumer level all the way to advocating for governmental changes.
Personal Food Waste
Personal or household food waste refers to the food that is discarded or wasted at home due to reasons such as overbuying, overcooking, forgetting about food stored in fridges and pantries, or simply not using all parts of the food item.
Addressing personal food waste requires individuals to take actions such as…
  • Planning meals
  • Buying only what is needed
  • Using up leftovers
  • Properly storing food
Retail and Industry Food Waste
Retail and industry food waste refers to the food that is discarded or wasted by manufacturers, processors, distributors, or retailers such as grocery stores, restaurants, and food service providers. This can occur for reasons such as overproduction, spoilage, cosmetic imperfections, or food that is not sold before its expiration date.
Addressing retail food waste requires corporations to take actions such as…
  • Improving supply chain management
  • Optimizing inventory
  • Implementing better storage and handling practices
  • Investing in more efficient processing technologies
  • Finding alternative uses for surplus food such as donating or composting
  • Offering initiatives such as discounts on food close to their expiration dates
Environmental Impacts
  • Greenhouse gas emissions:
    Food waste generates greenhouse gas emissions when it decomposes in landfills, releasing methane, a potent greenhouse gas that contributes to climate change.
  • Wasted resources:
    Producing and distributing food requires significant resources, including water, land, energy, and labor. When food is wasted, these resources are also wasted.
  • Water and land use:
    Agriculture and food production consume large amounts of water and land. When food is wasted, these resources are used inefficiently, leading to water and land degradation and reduced biodiversity.
  • Use of chemicals and pesticides:
    Food production and agriculture often rely on the use of chemicals and pesticides, which can have negative environmental impacts. When food is wasted, the use of these chemicals and pesticides is also wasted.
  • Reduced soil fertility:
    Food waste can reduce soil fertility by depleting nutrients and organic matter from the soil, leading to decreased productivity and increased erosion.
Economical Impacts
  • Loss of food resources and the associated costs of production, processing, and transportation
  • Increased disposal costs, including landfill fees and waste management expenses
  • Decreased profitability for businesses in the food production and retail sectors
  • Negative impact on the environment, leading to additional costs related to climate change and natural resource depletion
  • Loss of economic opportunities, such as donations to food banks or other charitable organizations
  • Impacts on global food security, particularly in developing countries with limited access to food
Social Impacts
  • Hunger and food insecurity:
    Food waste exacerbates global hunger and food insecurity by reducing the availability of food resources for people in need suffering from food insecurity or living in poverty.
  • Inequality and access to food:
    Food waste can also contribute to social inequality by limiting access to food for marginalized communities who live in areas where food access is limited.
  • Wasted resources:
    Food waste represents a waste of resources that could have been used for other social purposes, such as donating food to charities or investing in social programs that address issues like hunger and poverty.
  • Ethical concerns:
    Food waste raises ethical concerns related to the treatment of animals and the environmental impact of food production.
